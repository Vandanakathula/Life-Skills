# Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH)

This document outlines behaviors that constitute sexual harassment and the appropriate actions to take if such behavior is experienced or witnessed.

## Question 1: What Types of Behavior Constitute Sexual Harassment?

Sexual harassment refers to any unwelcome conduct of a sexual nature that creates an intimidating, hostile, degrading, or offensive environment.

It may include the following categories:

### 1. Physical Harassment

- Inappropriate or non-consensual touching  
- Attempted or forced physical intimacy  
- Physically blocking someone's movement  
- Any form of physical aggression with sexual intent  

### 2. Verbal Harassment

- Sexually colored remarks or jokes  
- Intrusive personal questions about private life  
- Repeated requests for dates or favors after refusal  
- Whistling, teasing sounds, or suggestive comments  
- Use of demeaning or offensive gender-based terms  

### 3. Visual and Digital Harassment

- Sexual gestures  
- Sharing or displaying explicit images, videos, or messages  
- Sending inappropriate digital content  
- Persistent or hostile staring  

### 4. Quid Pro Quo Harassment

- Threats of negative consequences for refusal  
- Promises of promotions, benefits, or preferential treatment in exchange for compliance  

## Question 2: What Actions Should Be Taken in Case of Such Behavior?

### If I Experience Harassment Personally

- Address the behavior immediately, if safe  
- Document details (date, time, location, witnesses)  
- Preserve digital evidence (emails, messages, screenshots)  
- File a formal complaint with the Internal Committee (IC)  

### If I Witness Harassment

Apply the **4 D’s of Bystander Intervention**:

- **Direct** – Intervene if safe  
- **Delegate** – Inform appropriate authority  
- **Document** – Record factual details  
- **Delay** – Support the affected individual afterward  

Additional actions:

- Offer support as a witness  
- Report the incident to the Internal Committee  

### In Case of Repeated Incidents

- Escalate the matter to the Internal Committee  
- Follow up to ensure corrective action  
- If internal resolution fails, escalate through external grievance mechanisms such as SHe-Box  
Every individual has the right to work in a safe, respectful, and inclusive environment. Preventing and addressing sexual harassment is a shared responsibility.
