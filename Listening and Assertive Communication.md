# 1. Active Listening.
## 1. What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?

1. **Stay mentally present**  
   Avoid distractions and focus fully on the speaker and the topic being discussed.

2. **Avoid interrupting**  
   Let the speaker complete their thoughts before responding to ensure they feel heard.

3. **Show engagement through body language**  
   Maintain eye contact, nod appropriately, and keep an open posture to show attentiveness.

4. **Take notes when needed**  
   During important discussions, notes help retain key information and demonstrate seriousness.

5. **Ask relevant follow-up questions**  
   Clarify doubts by asking respectful questions that deepen understanding.

6. **Avoid premature judgments**  
   Listen completely before forming opinions or conclusions.

7. **Respond thoughtfully**  
   Give responses that reflect understanding rather than reacting impulsively.


# 2. Reflective Listening
## 2. According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening? 

1. **Listen without interrupting**  
   Give the speaker your full attention and allow them to finish before you respond.

2. **Understand both words and feelings**  
   Try to understand not only what the person is saying, but also how they feel about it.

3. **Repeat the message in your own words**  
   Paraphrase what you heard to make sure you understood correctly.

4. **Confirm your understanding**  
   Check with the speaker to see if your understanding matches what they meant.

5. **Show empathy and respect**  
   Respond in a way that shows you value the speaker’s feelings and opinions.

6. **Avoid judging too quickly**  
   Focus first on understanding instead of immediately agreeing or disagreeing.

7. **Ask questions to clarify**  
   Politely ask questions if something is unclear instead of assuming.

8. **Summarize key points at the end**  
   Briefly restate the main points so everyone agrees on what was discussed.

# 3. Reflection
## 3. What are the obstacles in your listening process?

1. Sometimes I get distracted by my own thoughts while someone is speaking.
2. Noise or interruptions around me make it hard to focus properly.
3. I sometimes think about my reply before the speaker finishes talking.
4. Long or unclear explanations make me lose concentration.
5. Stress or tiredness also affects how well I listen.

## 4. What can you do to improve your listening?

1. I will focus fully on the speaker and avoid distractions during conversations.
2. I will let the speaker finish before thinking about my response.
3. I will ask questions when something is unclear.
4. I will summarize or repeat important points to confirm understanding.
5. I will practice patience and stay attentive throughout conversations.

# 4. Types Of Communication
## 5. When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day-to-day life?

1. When I want to avoid arguments or conflicts with others.
2. When I feel shy or less confident in a group discussion.
3. When talking to seniors or authority figures, I sometimes hesitate to express my opinion.
4. When I feel my opinion may not be important in the discussion.
5. When I want to maintain peace even if I disagree.

## 6. When do you switch into Aggressive communication style in your day-to-day life?

1. When I feel frustrated or stressed about something.
2. When I feel someone is not listening to me.
3. When deadlines or pressure make me impatient.
4. When I strongly disagree and react emotionally.
5. When I feel treated unfairly.

## 7. When do you switch into Passive-Aggressive communication style in your day-to-day life?

1. When I feel upset but don’t express it directly.
2. When I avoid confrontation but still feel angry.
3. When I use sarcasm or indirect comments instead of speaking openly.
4. When I stop talking or give short responses when annoyed.
5. When I complain to others instead of addressing the issue directly.

## 8. How can you make your communication assertive?

1. Speak clearly and confidently while respecting others opinions.
2. Express my thoughts and feelings honestly without hurting others.
3. Listen carefully to others before responding.
4. Stay calm even when disagreements happen.
5. Practice saying what I need or feel politely and directly.
